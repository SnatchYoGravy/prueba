Resumen Introduction: The Making of a Visionary My name is Dr. Paul Janssen, and my life has been a journey driven by an unrelenting passion for science, medicine, and the betterment of human health. Born on September 12, 1926, in Turnhout, Belgium, I grew up in a family where curiosity and intellectual pursuit were deeply valued. My father, Constant Janssen, was a physician, and his dedication to healing inspired me from a young age. Little did I know that this inspiration would lead me to become one of the most prolific pharmaceutical researchers in history, with over 80 medicines developed and more than 100 patents to my name. One of my earliest and most significant achievements was the development of haloperidol, an antipsychotic drug that transformed the treatment of schizophrenia. Before haloperidol, patients with severe mental illnesses had limited treatment options, often confined to asylums with little hope of recovery. Haloperidol offered them a chance at a better life, and it remains a cornerstone of psychiatric medicine to this day. Another milestone was the discovery of fentanyl, a powerful painkiller that has become essential in managing severe pain, particularly in surgical and cancer patients.
